\subsection{Article Similarity Scoring}

When a problem description is provided, articles are ranked by their semantic
similarity to the query. Each article receives a score based on the cosine
similarity between the query embedding and the stored abstract and title
embeddings:

\begin{equation}
    \text{score}_a = w_{\text{abs}} \cdot \text{sim}_{\text{abs}}(a)
                   + w_{\text{title}} \cdot \text{sim}_{\text{title}}(a)
    \label{eq:article-score}
\end{equation}

where $w_{\text{abs}} = 0.7$ and $w_{\text{title}} = 0.3$. The top $k = 50$
articles by this score are retained for the subsequent ranking step.

\subsection{Method Scoring}

For each candidate method, a score is accumulated from the top-$k$ articles
that mention it. The contribution of each article is based on its rank
position in two complementary rankings: one from the embedding-based similarity
search, and one from a cross-encoder reranker. Each unique (article, method)
pair contributes once, to avoid inflating scores when multiple method aliases
in a single article map to the same approach:

\begin{equation}
    \text{score}_m = \sum_{\substack{a \,\in\, \text{top-}k \\ m \,\in\, a}}
        \left(
            \frac{w_e}{r_e(a) + 1} + \frac{w_c}{r_c(a) + 1}
        \right)
    \label{eq:method-score}
\end{equation}

where $r_e(a)$ and $r_c(a)$ are the zero-based rank positions of article $a$
in the embedding ranking and cross-encoder ranking respectively, and $w_e$ and
$w_c$ are normalised weights with $w_e + w_c = 1$ (default $w_e = w_c = 0.5$).
Candidate methods are then sorted in descending order by $\text{score}_m$.

\subsection{Feedback Boost}

After similarity-based ranking, user feedback is used to adjust the final
ordering. Each method is assigned a smoothed feedback score using Bayesian
smoothing towards a neutral prior:

\begin{equation}
    \hat{r}_m = \frac{s \cdot \mu_0 + n \cdot \bar{r}_m}{s + n}
    \label{eq:smoothed-rating}
\end{equation}

where $\bar{r}_m$ is the observed mean rating, $n$ is the number of ratings,
$s = 5$ is the prior strength, and $\mu_0 = 3$ is the prior mean on a 1--5
scale. The smoothed rating is then normalised to $[-1, 1]$ relative to the
neutral rating of 3. The final combined score used to sort methods is:

\begin{equation}
    \text{combined\_score}(m) = \frac{1}{\text{rank}(m) + 1}
                              + w_f \cdot \text{feedback\_score}(m)
    \label{eq:combined-score}
\end{equation}

where $\text{rank}(m)$ is the zero-based position after similarity ranking
and $w_f = 0.15$.
